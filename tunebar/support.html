Tunebar Support

A simple menu bar music player for macOS.

Getting Started

Open Tunebar from your menu bar, click the folder icon to add a music folder, and your tracks will appear in the player. Supported formats include MP3, M4A, FLAC, OGG, AIFF, WAV, and AAC.

Editing Metadata

Right-click a track to edit its metadata or fetch it automatically from MusicBrainz. Metadata editing is available for MP3, M4A, FLAC, and OGG files.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why can't I edit metadata for some files?
    Only formats with reliable embedded metadata support (MP3, M4A, FLAC, OGG) allow editing. Formats like WAV, AIFF, and AAC do not have a dependable metadata specification.
  • Where are my files stored?
    Tunebar plays files directly from your chosen folder. It does not copy or move your music.
  • Does Tunebar use the internet?
    Only when you use "Fetch Metadata," which queries the MusicBrainz database. No other network activity occurs.
